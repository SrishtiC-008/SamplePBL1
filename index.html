What is a Stepwell

Wisit this website for more information. Stepwells. A stepwell is a well in which you descend a fleet of steps to reach the water. Some stepwells were considered religious, about 200 years ago.

Gachibowli Stepwell

The Gachi Baoli well is a landmark, and a prominent position in the city is named after it. Gachi denotes lime, whereas Baoli denotes water (baoli - means a well). Gachibowli is now a concrete jungle with corporate business parks containing IT firms, educational institutions, and densely packed residential neighbourhoods. The well is now hidden in the concrete jungle, abandoned, and devoid of its former grandeur.

Restoration of the stepwell

Members of The Rainwater Project, the group restoring the stepwells in Gachibowli and Kokapet, said the structures were being restored to their original grandeur.

“We see so many old heritage structures being cemented, but while restoring the Gachibowli stepwell, even the lime inside the well was tested before taking up structural alterations.

Ready mix with similar lime concentration was used while restoring,” said Kalpana Ramesh, founder of the forum.

“Chirec International, appreciating our commitment, decided to fund the project,” she added.

If you proceed to listen to our podcast you will see that Kalpana Ramesh is one of our interviewees.

What is Hyderabad doing to restore these stepwells

The city is seeing the revival of step-wells. Heritage step-wells in the city and in neighbouring areas are being identified and restored in a phased manner. The step-wells that are vital to retaining the groundwater levels apart from preserving the rich heritage of the city are being revived and restored by the state government, with city-based volunteers doing their best by putting in their efforts.

What can we do to restore these stepwells

Principal Secretary of Municipal Administration and Urban Development Arvind Kumar recently has tweeted a photo of a step-well in Gudimalkapur being cleaned and restored to its original look. He added that the Bhagwan Das step-well cleaning work was taken up by the HMDA and the heritage admirers can look forward to visit it and take photographs. So we do our part to volunteer to help clean up the stepwells and restore it to it's full potential.
